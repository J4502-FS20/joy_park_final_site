Pumpkins, Spice and Fall, Oh My!

/pəm(p)kən/

noun

1. A large rounded orange-yellow fruit with a thick rind, edible flesh, and many seeds.

2. The plant of the gourd family that produces pumpkins, having tendrils and large lobed leaves and native to warm regions of America.

It's no secret that pumpkins are an American obsession and a seasonal fall icon. Every year, it seems that pumpkin spice foods grow more and more omnipresent. Within the past decade, pumpkin production increased 53% from $117 million to $180 million, according to the U.S. Department of Agriculture. The pumpkin market, albeit limited and seasonal, has turned into a new, profitable industry due to the many demands they fulfill, from pie-making and pumpkin spiced goods to pumpkin carving and pumpkin patches.
