Pumpkins, spice and fall, oh my!

/pəm(p)kən/

noun

1. a large rounded orange-yellow fruit with a thick rind, edible flesh, and many seeds.

2. the plant of the gourd family that produces pumpkins, having tendrils and large lobed leaves and native to warm regions of America.

It's no secret that pumpkins are an American obsession and a seasonal icon each time fall rolls around. Each year, more and more brands come out with limited edition pumpkin products that go beyond our wildest imaginations, like Kraft’s pumpkin spice mac and cheese. Grab a PSL at your nearest coffee shop and browse around to learn more about America's pumpkin craze and how it became the flavor of fall.
